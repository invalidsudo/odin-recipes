Miyeokguk

Description

Miyeokguk is a Korean soup traditionally consumed on one's birthday.

"Miyeok" means "seaweed" in Korean, and "guk" means "soup." So, "miyeokguk" is "seaweed soup."

Ingredients

  • 1 cup beef
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup dried seaweed
  • 1 L rinsed rice water
  • 1 L tap water
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1-2 tbsp fish sauce (to taste)

Steps

  1. Grab the beef and put it in a bowl.
  2. Add the black pepper and some soy sauce to the beef.
  3. Mince the garlic and add it to the beef.
  4. Use hands to mix everything together evenly.
  5. Let beef marinate for 15 mins.
  6. Grab the seaweed and put it in another bowl.
  7. Add tap water.
  8. Let the seaweed sit for 30 mins.
  9. Wash the seaweed.
  10. Form the seaweed into a ball and chop it into thirds.
  11. Chop the beef into small pieces.
  12. Add sesame oil to pot.
  13. Add beef on medium heat. Cook until mostly brown.
  14. Add kosher salt and the rest of the soy sauce.
  15. Add rinsed rice water.
  16. Add fish sauce.
  17. Simmer for 20-30 mins.
  18. Serve with a bowl of rice.
